Brownie: Automation for the Elderly

Fall 2017

Problem

Senior citizens are increasingly projected to live on their own

They may need the help of their children or caretakers, the latter of which may:

  • Be expensive to hire
  • Be difficult to find
  • Deprive the elderly of a sense of independence

Idea

A comprehensive smart home system catered for the elderly!

Challenges

Such a system would need to tackle the following challenges posed by the target audience, to name a few:

  • Limited mobility
  • Physical disabilities or impairment
  • Discomfort with technology
  • Emergencies such as falling accidents

Competitor Analysis

Cross-examined related companies and products

HoneyCo | Samsung SmartThings | Crestron | Lively Activity Sensors

Purpose was to identify their strengths and weaknesses and find areas for opportunity

HoneyCo came the closest to paralleling what we trying to accomplish but lacked user-friendliness and comprehensiveness

Contextual Inquiry

To design a smart home system that best served the needs of the older population, we had to understand the following:

  • How they lived their daily lives and what difficulties they faced
  • Their health conditions and problems
  • Their interactions with and attitude toward high-tech devices
  • How their adult children played a role

Interviews

Three older adults between the ages of 61 and 70, as well as three younger adults between the ages of 43 and 46, interviewed

Seniors generally less comfortable with tech

Privacy considered very important by all

Health seen as major concern

For the most part frequent communication between parents and children

Survey

Online survey conducted for adults 35 and up

Respondents who were 55+ reported lower trust level for automation and lower comfort with technology

Most respondents valued privacy, security, safety, health monitoring

Opportunity

Demand for automation services was low among elderly but could be ascribed to unfamiliarity with technology in general

Our aim was to to be able to bridge this gap

Our solution would be user-friendly and useful for seniors

Affinity Diagram

Main points from interviews and surveys collated, then sorted into categories: Automation, Reminder, Health, Contact, and Other

Personas

We created personas, who embodied the voice of our interview and survey participants, to help guide our design process

Frank: A 68-year-old who needs a solution for tackling his forgetfulness and bonding with his family

Vickie: A 42-year-old who wants to have her mother taken care of with the assistance of technology
